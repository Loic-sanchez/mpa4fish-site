## Overview

MP4Fish (Molecular Physiology, Population Genetics & Sustainable Management of Fish) is a four-year collaborative research project funded by the European Commission under the Horizon Europe framework. The project started in January 2024 and unites expertise from marine biology, molecular ecology, genomics, bioinformatics, and fisheries science.

The project targets several ecologically and commercially important fish species in European Atlantic and Arctic waters, including Atlantic cod (*Gadus morhua*), European anchovy (*Engraulis encrasicolus*), and European eel (*Anguilla anguilla*).

---

## Objectives

**WP1 — Molecular stress physiology**
Characterise the molecular responses of target species to thermal stress, hypoxia, and contaminants using transcriptomics and proteomics approaches.

**WP2 — Population genomics**
Reconstruct the population structure, connectivity, and demographic history of target species using whole-genome sequencing and environmental DNA (eDNA) metabarcoding.

**WP3 — Local adaptation and climate vulnerability**
Identify genomic signatures of local adaptation and model species' vulnerability to projected climate scenarios.

**WP4 — Tools for sustainable management**
Develop genomic-informed stock assessment methods and provide actionable recommendations to fisheries managers and policy makers.

**WP5 — Dissemination, training & open science**
Ensure all project outputs are openly accessible, and train the next generation of marine scientists through summer schools and secondments.

---

## Scientific Approach

MP4Fish combines cutting-edge genomic technologies with long-term ecological datasets and advanced statistical modelling. All data produced within the project will be made publicly available through established repositories (NCBI, PANGAEA, GBIF) in compliance with the FAIR data principles.

---

## Expected Impacts

- Improved understanding of how European fish populations are adapting to climate change
- New genomic markers available for routine stock identification
- Open-source bioinformatics pipelines for population genomics in non-model fish species
- Policy briefs and recommendations submitted to ICES and the European Commission
- Training of 12 early-career researchers across 6 countries
